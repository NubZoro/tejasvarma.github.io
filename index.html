The Journal

ORIGIN // HYDERABAD
How I Got Here

Always been drawn to stories and other worlds — as a kid, books were the escape. Then games took over for the same reason: you don't just watch the world, you live in it. That obsession quietly built up over years. Then some friends dragged me into a game jam, just for fun. I didn't expect much. What I found was something completely different — not just playing games, but pulling them apart. Understanding what makes every frame, every sound, every mechanic feel the way it does. That was it. Ever since, it's been a journey to learn everything — not for any goal in particular, just for the love of it. And somewhere along the way, to make experiences that help others the same way games helped me.

CURRENT MISSION
The Club

Leads the Shader GameDev Subcommittee at Enigma, but the goal was always bigger than a technical CS club. Built it to be a space for everyone — programmers, designers, artists, writers, musicians, beginners, veterans, or anyone who just really loves video games. A place where you don't need credentials to belong. Workshops, game jams, mentorship — all of it designed so that the door is open to all, not just people who already know how to code.

CRAFT PHILOSOPHY
What I Actually Do

Work across the full stack — code, 3D art, level design, pixel art, music, SFX, narrative, UI. Not because it's required, but because every layer informs every other. The way a sound lands changes how a mechanic feels. The way a space is lit changes how a story reads. Right now actively trying to consume and learn as much as possible across all of it — tools, techniques, theory, history. Not building toward a destination. Just endlessly chasing interesting ideas and wanting to see them actually exist in the world.

WHAT DRIVES IT
Taste & Obsession

Genuinely obsessed with narratives, storytelling, cinematography, and animation — across books, films, and games. But games especially, because you don't just watch a story unfold — you live inside it. You make choices. You get lost. You wake up at 3am because you couldn't put the controller down. That feeling of being completely absorbed into another world is unlike anything else. And the obsession underneath everything is to one day build one of those worlds. One that someone else can disappear into, escape to, and carry with them long after the credits roll.

01Disco Elysium
Every frame a painting. Every NPC felt real. I formed actual bonds with the residents of Revachol. The music, the art, the environment design — a masterpiece of craft.

My first AAA game. An absolute visual spectacle. Night City at 3am with the windows down is still something I chase. The mission design and freedom of approach changed what I think games can be.

Played it 5 times — solo and with friends — and I'm still finding quests I've never seen. It doesn't just let you play a story, it lets you make one. Pure immersion.

Short, and near-perfect. BT is my GOAT. The level design innovations still blow my mind — the movement, gunplay, and the shift between pilot and mech is chef's kiss.
05Inscryption
Started my roguelike obsession. A card game inside a horror puzzle inside a meta rabbit hole about found footage. Every time you think it's over, it becomes something new.

The closest thing to a perfect game. Music? Phenomenal. World design? Stunning. The dodge-parry mechanic on hard mode is pure dopamine. Every character synergy feels like a discovery.

One of my very first PC games. The hacking mechanic was like nothing else at the time. Aiden Pearce's arc is genuinely well-written and still holds up.

One of the earliest sci-fi films I watched, and I've been addicted to the genre ever since. It's what got me genuinely interested in tech — the idea that reality itself could be a system to understand and manipulate.

Completed Missions

◆ COMPLETEUE5 // BLENDER PLAY ↗
Outpass — College Horror

Made for a game jam. The environment is entirely hand-modelled after our actual college dorms — familiar geometry turned unsettling. Collaborated with friends who handled voice acting, music, and parts of the code. The real challenge and learning here was lighting: how it shapes horror, how it interacts with environment materials, and how much mood lives in the shadows rather than what you show. Not a perfect result, but one of the most educational projects I've shipped.

◆ SOLOGBSTUDIO PLAY ↗
Bad Trip — GameBoy RPG

Our club's orientation ran a "Retro" theme — retro consoles, Raspberry Pis, old CRTs. So I made a game that actually ran on a GameBoy, in 2 days. The real design puzzle was working within its hard constraints: 4-colour palette, tile limits, sprite pixel caps, tiny map sizes, and heavily restricted audio. Every asset, SFX, and music track made solo. Runs differently depending on the emulator or device.

◆ COMPLETEUNITY PLAY ↗
Requiem — Roguelike Rhythm

Built in 3 days for a jam themed on "Escalation". As you level up and upgrade abilities, the music tempo actually escalates — making the rhythm game harder the stronger you get. On top of that, enemies scale in the classic roguelike way. Two escalation systems feeding each other: the better you play, the more demanding it becomes. Every asset and track is original. Made with a friend.

◆ SOLO PROTOTYPEUNITY PLAY ↗
Schmovement — Movement Lab

Not a game — a personal sandbox for movement mechanics. Wall running, sliding, and a web-swing style grapple hook that prioritises momentum and arc over simple rope physics. Built solo to deeply understand what makes movement feel good rather than just function correctly. Ongoing testbed for ideas.

◆ UNFINISHEDUNITY PLAY ↗
Soulbound — HD-2D Prototype

An experiment into the HD-2D / 2.5D style — placing 2D sprites inside a full 3D environment. The visual layering and lighting interactions of flat characters in volumetric space fascinated me. Built with a friend but left unfinished due to time constraints. The core of what makes the style interesting is in there.
